RESEARCH INSIGHTS
RQ1

Adaptation Effect

Increasing alpha from 0 to 0.4 increased target fixation from 0.312 to 0.763 (+145%). Compromise counts remained stable (8.45→8.46), suggesting adaptation changed attacker behaviour without significantly improving outcomes.

RQ2

Defender Capability

Patch Speed (r=0.35 with resilience) is substantially more important than Detection Rate (r=−0.04). Invest in patching speed before detection improvements for best security ROI.

RQ3

Risk Combinations

Attacker Skill=0.9 produces 0.31–0.32 attack success rate regardless of detection rate. Skill dominates all other factors — the scenario heatmap confirms skill drives rows, not columns.

RQ4

ML Performance

XGBoost R²=0.7189, RMSE=0.0603. 5-fold CV R²: 0.7204 ± 0.0051. Low standard deviation confirms stability and no overfitting. Trained on 18,225 BehaviorSpace simulation runs.
